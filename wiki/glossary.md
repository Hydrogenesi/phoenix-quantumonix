## Geometric Terms

### Convergence Envelope
Bounded interior region within which all stable recursive paths must remain.
- Shrinks with depth: `r(D) = r₀ × ρ^D`
- Exiting envelope leads to instability
- Typically triadic, spherical, or ellipsoidal geometry

### Geometric Fidelity
Preservation of ratios, angles, and relationships across recursive scales.
- High fidelity: ≥ 95% (required for stable recursion)
- Moderate: 85–95% (functional but less stable)
- Low: < 85% (risk of divergence)

### Topological Continuity
Preservation of structural connectivity across recursive transformations.
No breaks, no jumps, no tears in state space.
